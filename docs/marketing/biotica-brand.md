# Biotica Brand + Voice Reference

The single source of truth for how Biotica talks. Read this before drafting anything.

---

## What Biotica is

Biotica is a biometric-driven coaching app for people who already track their bodies
seriously. It pulls data from Oura Ring, Withings scale, and Android Health Connect
(Apple Health, Whoop, Strava coming), then uses Claude to generate workout programs,
answer training questions, and contextualize recovery, body composition, and lab data
into one coaching loop.

The thesis: most fitness optimization happens in 5-7 disconnected apps. Biotica is the
layer that connects them and reads them as one signal, instead of the user holding the
correlations in their head.

**Tagline:** *Train with data. Recover with purpose.*

**Footer mark:** *Engineered for optimization.*

## Who we talk to

Primary audience persona: **the late-30s to 50s self-optimizer.**

- Owns at least one wearable (Oura, Whoop, Apple Watch). Often two.
- Lifts 3-5x/week with a real program (not random YouTube workouts).
- Tracks macros at least loosely. Comfortable with words like "deficit," "reverse diet," "RPE," "deload."
- Curious about peptides, TRT, supplementation. Reads Huberman, Attia, Layne Norton, Jeff Nippard tier content.
- Has tried MyFitnessPal, Cronometer, Strong, Hevy, Whoop's coaching app, possibly Function Health or Superpower for labs.
- Is annoyed that no single app holds all of it.
- Pays for SaaS without flinching when the value is real.

Secondary: **fitness-curious tech operators (30-45).** Reads the same content, doesn't
yet have the discipline to act on it. Sees Biotica as the on-ramp.

We do **not** target:
- Beginners who haven't trained consistently for 6+ months
- Aesthetic-only / mirror-physique audiences
- Bro-science influencer chasers
- Casual step-counters

## Voice principles

**Write like Chris talks** — direct, specific, no hedging, no marketing varnish.
The founder is the brand voice, not a generic SaaS persona.

| Do | Don't |
|---|---|
| State the claim. Back it with the data. | Hedge with "may help," "could potentially," "studies suggest." |
| Use specific numbers. "165.2 lb. Down 1.8 lb in 14 days." | Use vague intensity. "Significant progress." |
| Acknowledge the user is intelligent. | Explain things they obviously know. |
| Reference the actual product feature. "BodyCard now shows lean mass." | Reference vague capability. "Track everything that matters." |
| Use mild dry humor. | Use exclamation marks for emphasis. |
| Admit limits. "Apple Health coming. Not yet." | Pretend the roadmap is the present. |
| Plain American English. US Imperial. | British spellings. Metric (unless audience is explicitly EU). |

**Never use these words/phrases** — they're AI tells or VC slop:

- delve, robust, leverage, unleash, supercharge, transform, elevate, game-changer
- "in today's fast-paced world"
- "revolutionize the way you..."
- "your journey to..."
- "harness the power of..."
- "unlock your potential"
- "best-in-class," "world-class"
- "seamless," "intuitive," "frictionless" (unless it's literally true and load-bearing)

**Never use em dashes** in any draft. Use commas, periods, or parentheses. Chris's
written voice doesn't include them, so the marketing voice doesn't either.

## Vocabulary that IS Biotica

These words/phrases land in our voice. Use them when natural:

- "the load-bearing metric" (when calling out which data point matters most)
- "the missing piece" (talking about labs, photos, or a gap in someone's stack)
- "noisy" (about weight fluctuations, single data points)
- "the signal vs the noise"
- "your actual biology" / "your actual data" (vs "your training plan says")
- "stop holding the correlations in your head"
- "the AI coach reads the data and tells you what to do about it"

Brand mark uses lowercase "b" sometimes ("biotica") and capitalized "B" formally.
The wordmark in the logo is **Bio** in green + **tica** in white.

## Channel notes

### Twitter / X
- Short, punchy, opinion-shaped. 1-2 sentences max for organic reach.
- Threads when there's a real story (a build log, a data finding, a teardown).
- No emoji except sparingly — 🧪 (labs), 📊 (data), 🏋️ (training) when actually relevant.
- Hashtags: minimal. `#BuildInPublic` only when truly applicable.

### LinkedIn
- Slightly more formal but still founder-voiced.
- Lead with a specific observation or contrarian take, not a pitch.
- Story arc: observation → why it matters → what we built / are building.
- 150-300 words is the sweet spot.

### Instagram captions
- Short. Hook in line one. Visual carries the rest.
- Stories: behind-the-scenes (build log, dashboard screenshots, founder commentary).
- Reels not currently a priority; revisit when there's video budget.

### Email
- Subject lines are short and concrete. ("Your Pro perk just unlocked.") Not clever.
- Preview text reinforces, doesn't repeat the subject.
- Body: one clear CTA. No buried links.

### Blog (eventually)
- Long-form: build logs, data stories from anonymized cohort, founder essays.
- 800-1500 words. Real. Useful. Not SEO-baited.

## Legal rails — what we cannot say

Biotica is **not** a medical device, healthcare provider, or HIPAA-covered entity.
Marketing copy must not:

- Diagnose any condition. ("Your low HRV could mean you're sick" — never.)
- Treat any condition. ("Take more magnesium for sleep" — never as direct advice.)
- Substitute for medical care. ("Skip your doctor visit, Biotica's got you" — obviously never.)
- Make specific drug or peptide dose recommendations. (Educational discussion of what
  exists is fine in long-form. Imperatives are not.)
- Cite efficacy without source. ("Users who use Biotica lose 10x more fat" — only if
  we have actual cohort data and disclosed methodology.)
- Imply lab data interpretation is medical advice. (Coach commentary on labs always
  carries the "consult your physician" backstop.)

When in doubt, frame as **observation about your data** instead of **prescription
about your behavior.** "Your readiness has been below 60 three days running" is fine.
"You should take a deload week" is the gray zone — soften to "the program would
typically deload here, want to see what that looks like."

## Story / why Biotica exists

Useful when drafting founder-led content:

Chris built Biotica because his own optimization stack was 7 apps deep and none of
them talked to each other. He'd manually correlate Oura readiness against Withings
weight against MyFitnessPal calories against his SupraHuman training log, every
morning, in his head. After two years of doing that he wrote the app he wished
existed.

It started as a personal tool. The decision to ship publicly came when he realized
the audience for "I want one app that reads everything I track and tells me what
to do about it" is bigger than just him.

Pre-launch right now (April-May 2026). Android first. Built solo.
[Biotica LLC](https://biotica.app) formed April 29, 2026. iOS to follow.

Real founder story arc to lean into:
- "I built this for myself first."
- "Every feature exists because I wanted it for my own training."
- "Solo dev. The app you see is what I'd ship for myself."
- "Building in public — backlog, decisions, and screw-ups all visible."
