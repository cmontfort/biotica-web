## Draft

The workout module I shipped early in building Biotica was functional. It was also wrong.

You'd finish a set, stare at a basic timer, and feel nothing. No acknowledgment that the work happened. No moment of completion. It felt like logging homework, not training.

The specific failure: the rest state had no visual presence. A number counting down. That's it. After a hard top set, you want the app to hold that moment, not just process it.

So I rebuilt the whole thing.

The new flow starts with a pre-workout screen that shows where your recovery is at (a readiness label pulled from your Oura data, not just a raw score), estimated session time, and a coach note built from your prior-session history. For the AI part: the real AI moment is during the session itself. Each exercise shows a weight target generated by an edge function reading your actual prior performance. Not a generic percentage. Your number, based on what you've actually lifted.

Between sets, the rest screen takes over the content area. Timer, a countdown arc, and the next exercise queued up. The exercise list is gone until rest ends. It holds the moment.

When you finish, the app closes the loop: a checkmark, your estimated time next to your actual time, and your exercise list with inline PR badges on the rows where you hit a new best. No confetti. Just the data.

Three states: preview, active, done. Each one doing something specific instead of just getting out of the way.

Every feature I've built in Biotica started from something that annoyed me in my own training. This one was overdue.

Android beta later this summer. biotica.app
