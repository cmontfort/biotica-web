# Biotica Voice Examples

Sample posts in Biotica's voice across formats and topics. The marketing-project
agent should mimic the cadence, structure, and word choice here, not just the
information.

When you find a post out in the wild that lands in Biotica's voice, paste it in
here. The library is the iteration loop.

## LinkedIn

**Founder essay — why an LLC matters:**

> Formed Biotica LLC last week.
>
> A solo-built fitness app didn't strictly need an entity to exist. It needed one to do anything serious — sign a B2B lab integration, publish under a business App Store account, take payments through a real Stripe account, separate personal liability from a product that surfaces health-adjacent recommendations.
>
> The lesson I keep relearning: the boring infrastructure work unblocks the interesting product work. The LLC doesn't ship features. It ships the ability to ship features.
>
> Apple Developer enrollment, Junction lab integration, Stripe production keys, all gated on the entity existing. Six weeks ago I'd have called this stuff a distraction. Now I see it for what it is — load-bearing.
>
> If you're solo and pre-launch, file the LLC before you think you need it. The day you DO need it, you can't wait two weeks for it.

**Product-thinking post — choosing what NOT to build:**

> Biotica integrates with Oura, Withings, Health Connect, soon Junction labs.
>
> It does NOT integrate with MyFitnessPal. We had it working. We removed it.
>
> The MFP API has been deprecated for years. The web scraping path triggers Cloudflare blocks. Maintaining either route would mean breaking 10% of users every month and apologizing on Twitter.
>
> The lesson: an integration that's flaky is worse than no integration at all. Users blame the app, not the API.
>
> Better to ship Cronometer or Nutritionix later, properly, than keep MFP limping along.

## Email

**Subject lines:**

- "Your Pro perk just unlocked." (transactional)
- "Lean mass is on the body card." (feature launch)
- "What your weight didn't tell you this week." (weekly brief preview)
- "Six things shipped to Biotica this week." (weekly digest)

**Don't write subject lines like:**

- ❌ "🚀 You won't BELIEVE what we just shipped!"
- ❌ "Your fitness journey, transformed."
- ❌ "Unlock the next level of your training."

## Anti-examples

What to NEVER ship under the Biotica name:

- ❌ "🌟 Biotica revolutionizes how you train! 💪 Unleash your potential with AI-powered coaching that delves into your biometrics 🚀"
- ❌ "Transform your fitness journey with our seamless, intuitive, best-in-class biometric platform!"
- ❌ "Biotica leverages cutting-edge AI to elevate your performance to the next level."
- ❌ "Discover the game-changing way smart athletes are optimizing their bodies."
- ❌ Any post that opens with "In today's fast-paced world..."

If a draft uses any of those phrases, it's wrong. Reject the draft. Try again.
