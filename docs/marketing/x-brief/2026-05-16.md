### Target 1 — @PeterAttiaMD

**Find:** Peter's last 90 days have leaned hard on strength training as the
load-bearing healthspan intervention. Look for a thread on grip strength, leg
press benchmarks, the "stability decade" (40s-50s), or any reply chain off his
podcast clips on muscle mass and longevity. Anything on the gap between cardio
fitness and muscular strength as separate longevity inputs is a clean entry.

**Best fit angle for Biotica:** Peter frames training as preventative medicine
for the 80-year-old version of yourself. The implementation gap is the same
gap Biotica solves: people lift, but they have no way to see whether the work
is actually producing the adaptation. Strength numbers in one app. Body comp
in another. Recovery in a third. The arc only shows up when you put them on
the same axis.

**Reply scaffold — if the thread is about strength training as longevity intervention:**

> The case for strength training in your 40s and 50s is settled. The harder
> question is whether your training is actually building the thing.
>
> Lifting four days a week with no rolling read on lean mass and no read on
> session-over-session load progression is just exercise. The intervention
> only counts if the adaptation lands. That part lives in the data, not in
> the program.

**Reply scaffold — if the thread is about grip strength / functional strength benchmarks:**

> Benchmarks are useful only against your own curve. Grip at 40 versus grip at
> 50 in the same person tells you something. Grip at 40 versus a population
> chart tells you almost nothing.
>
> The cheap version of this is logging the number monthly next to bodyweight
> and lean mass. The trend is the story. The single reading is noise.

>

**Reply scaffold — if the thread is about exercise selection (machine vs free weight, etc.):**

> The exercise-selection question I keep coming back to is which variant
> costs you the least in fatigue for the same stimulus.
>

> spine-loading tax that costs you on Friday's deadlift. Same quad stimulus
> on a hack with none of the tax. You only see the tradeoff in the recovery
> data the next day.
